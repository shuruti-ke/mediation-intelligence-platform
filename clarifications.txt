2. Jitsi/Jibri: Operational Overhead vs. Cost
Self-hosting Jitsi + Jibri gives you data sovereignty (great for trust), but:
Consider a hybrid approach for MVP: Use a managed Jitsi provider (like 8x8.vc or a hosted Jitsi on AWS) for Phase 1–2 to reduce DevOps load, then migrate to self-hosted once you have revenue.
Jibri requires dedicated VMs (4 CPU, 8GB RAM) — factor this into your cloud cost projections. If recording isn't critical for MVP, could it be a Phase 2+ premium feature?
Design your schema with migration in mind (e.g., avoid heavy reliance on SQLite-specific features).
When you hit scale or need advanced Postgres features (RLS, complex JSONB queries), Hyperdrive + Neon is a smooth transition.
4. AI/RAG Costs: Guardrails for Profitability
AI features (knowledge base, judiciary search) are powerful but can get expensive:
Implement query rate limits per tenant based on subscription tier.
Cache frequent judiciary search results to avoid repeated API calls to Tausi/Laws.Africa.
Consider making advanced AI features (e.g., "AI-assisted settlement drafting") a premium add-on.
5. Privacy & Compliance: Commercial ≠ Casual
Even though this isn't government, your users (especially in Kenya/EU) will expect strong privacy:
Your plan mentions consent_flags — excellent. Ensure your UI makes consent explicit and auditable.
6. Document Handling: Watch PDF/OCR Costs
Apache Tika is powerful but Java-heavy. For MVP:
Start with PyMuPDF (PDF) + python-docx (DOCX) for the 80% use case.
Add Tika later if you truly need 1000+ format support — it adds operational complexity.
7. Go-to-Market Alignment
Phase 4's "Public Portal" is great for user acquisition. Consider adding a free tier (e.g., 1 free mediation session) to drive adoption.
The "Should I Mediate?" assessment could double as a lead-gen tool — capture emails/phone numbers (with consent) for follow-up.
